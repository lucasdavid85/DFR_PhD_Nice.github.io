Project Overview

This page focuses on the structural modeling and bioinformatic analysis of the DFR enzyme, a key component in the flavonoid biosynthesis pathway.

Research Objectives

  • Predict 3D structure of DFR using AlphaFold and SwissModel
  • Perform molecular docking with substrates (e.g., dihydroquercetin)
  • Run molecular dynamics simulations to assess enzyme flexibility
  • Conduct in silico mutagenesis of key catalytic residues

Tools and Techniques

  • AlphaFold for structure prediction
  • AutoDock Vina for ligand docking
  • GROMACS for molecular dynamics simulation
  • PyMOL and Chimera for visualization

Expected Insights

Through this analysis, we aim to understand the substrate specificity, cofactor binding, and conformational dynamics that govern the catalytic efficiency of DFR in flavonoid biosynthesis.
